Post-Rock instrumental émotionnel et positif Math/Post-rock from Paris, France

Description
Créé en 2012, le groupe formé de Adrien à la batterie, Valentin et Julian aux guitares et Julien à la basse, vogue entre un univers post-rock et math-rock. Formed in 2012, Man Is Not A Bird consists of Adrien (drums), Valentin and Julian (guitars), and Julien (bass). Their style blends post-rock ambiances with math-rock rythms.

Bio

Passionate about infography and science-fiction, Julian, who was playing guitar in Parisian rock band "Mathilda" started his first music side-project in 2008, "Alastra", blending Hardcore/metal riffs and vocals with vintage synthetizers and ambient sounds. When Adrien joined as the new drummer of Alastra, in 2011, and after the split up of Mathilda, Julian's compositions took a more melancholic path. He started "Odds & Candor", a screamo/post-rock project with Valentin, guitarist of electro-pop band "D'Austerlitz". Although their project did not last long after their first gig, Julian and Valentin had found each other as musical partners in crime.

From the ashes of both side-projects, Adrien, Julian and Valentin ran into each other at a "And So I Watch You From Afar" concert, and everything became clear: they needed to blend their post-rock inspiration with happier rythms and catchy melodies. After two weeks of jams with Julien (bass), Man Is Not A Bird convinced the audience at their first gig, then started to be invited to open for several emo/math/post-rock gigs in small/underground Parisian venues. Despite the choice of keeping their music purely instrumental, their songs and live performances touched wider audiences than the post-rock and math-rock niche communities. Probably thanks to their catchy melodies and powerful progressive stories that they could fit in a regular-length song. This enthusiasm led them to opening for Kwoon in an unusually classy venue for post-rock concerts: le Divan du Monde, Paris.

In a few days, Man Is Not A Bird, is going to release their new song "Sounds of spring", mastered by Amaury Sauvé, in a split-album with French post-rock band "Puzzle".
